In Class...

CLose up of Bubble wrap from Week 1

In Week 1's Tutorial, we were tasked with sketching speculative HCIs around a given random object. For our group, we were given bubble wrap. Before starting our brainstorm, we considered the qualities of bubble wrap and concluded with the following: Bubble wrap can trap air, can pop with a satisfying sound, the space inside can potentially be filled and the plastic material is clear.

By considering the qualities of Bubble wrap, we thus came up with the following ideas for how to make it interactive:

  • A portable water container for travel purposes.
  • New packaging for condiments. (we noticed how each space looks roughly equal to 1Tbsp)
  • Amenities travel pack (three pack with shampoo, conditioner and body wash included)
  • Bubble wrap keyboard
  • An inner system where an interaction with any bubble will cause the rest of the bubbles to light up in a ripple effect (Party Time!)
  • Can be used as a method to mail scents to people? (store the scent of Paris while on holiday and then send it to someone back home)

Post Class....

The Day I couldnt write a poem.

'The day I couldnt write a poem' is a website where the creator, Zennyan, tells a story of how he struggled to write a poem with 'lovely words' and 'lovely imagery' through the form of a game where the user walk on texts. Throughout the game, the user interacts with the story through a range of different obstacles that align with the story's meaning (the author's experience of not being able to write poetry). For instance, as the reader walks over the words 'pop up in my mind', the word 'pop' will literally pop and shoot the user upwards.

To interact with website, the user only uses the left and right arrow keys to move as well as the up key to jump.

Reading Machine.

Wen Zhuang's 'Portraits and Repetition' on the website Reading Machines is an interesting interactive website where the user watches letters of a paragraph fall slowly, with the speed increasing as more letters fall. The paragraph seems to be describing 'Adrian, who you see naked', possibly thus drawing a portrait of him? (since lots of art models are not clothed). As the letters keep falling whilst increasing in speed, the sound design of this website also increases, evoking feelings of panic and surprise in the user.

Once the letter has fallen from the paragraph, the user also has the option to click and control the letter however they like. By pressing the left and right arrow keys, the user also can change between the pages. Each page consists of the same interactions however the paragraph about 'Adrian' is just slightly different. He is described the same however some small details such as '…you saw naked' might change to 'I saw naked'… thus hinting at the 'repetition' in the works name.

Crazy 8s

A crazy8s was conducted to quickly brainstorm some ideas that we could later on use for our web-based workbook. The ideas that I ended up really liking were the detective pin up board and the camera one. I thought the camera idea would be a bit hard to do without the use of complicated Javascript as I envisioned multiple instances of animation as well as access to the desktop camera. I thus decided to tweak it slightly to reference a digital camera screen, with videos playing in the background to simulate the taking of a photo on a camera. The detective pin up board was another idea I wanted to keep exploring. The initial idea was that the post its on the board would be draggable by the user and the red lines on the board will also be drawable by the user. This will thus simulate like the user has been put into the shoes of a detective, making connections between photos to determine a culprit.
